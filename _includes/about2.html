Web tracking techniques are quite varied and can use a myriad of different methods to access and store data on the users of a site or service. In general, the majority of tracked data is generated through cookies and other data that is stored locally on a user's machine by sites they visit. This cookie data is essentially a map of all locations that a user has visited, and in some cases can contain other personal information. If a site or analytic software gains access to this cookie data, they can begin to develop a profile on the activities of the user.
